iPhone Instructions

Add the Work Order App
to Your iPhone

Follow these steps to install the app on your home screen and enable notifications. It takes about two minutes.

Important: These steps must be done in Safari. Chrome and other browsers on iPhone will not work for this installation.
1
Open Safari and Go to the Site
Open the Safari browser on your iPhone. In the address bar at the top, type chs-cs.com/workorder and tap Go.
Screenshot: Safari address bar showing chs-cs.com/workorder
2
Log In With Your School Account
Sign in using your Warrick County Google account (the same one you use for school email). You should see your work order dashboard after signing in.
Screenshot: Google sign-in screen
3
Tap the Share Button
At the bottom of the Safari screen, look for the Share button — it looks like a small box with an arrow pointing upward out of it. Tap it once.

If you don't see the bottom toolbar, try scrolling up slightly on the page — it will reappear.

Screenshot: Safari bottom toolbar with Share button highlighted
4
Tap "Add to Home Screen"
A menu will slide up from the bottom of your screen. Scroll down through the options until you see "Add to Home Screen" and tap it.

It won't be the first option — scroll past your contacts and apps. Look for it listed with a plus (+) icon.

Screenshot: Share sheet with "Add to Home Screen" option highlighted
5
Tap "Add" to Confirm
A screen will appear showing the app name. You can leave the name as-is. Tap "Add" in the upper right corner to finish.
Screenshot: "Add to Home Screen" confirmation screen
6
Open the App From Your Home Screen
Go to your iPhone home screen and find the Work Orders icon. Always open the app this way — using the home screen icon instead of Safari. Tap it now to continue setup.
Screenshot: iPhone home screen with Work Orders icon
7
Enable Push Notifications
When the app opens, you'll see a dark blue banner near the top that says "Enable push notifications." Tap that banner — your iPhone will then ask if you want to allow notifications. Tap "Allow."

This is what lets the app alert you when a work order needs your attention — even when the app isn't open.

Screenshot: Notification banner in app + "Allow Notifications" iPhone prompt
