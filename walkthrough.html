Work Order Management System — Application Walkthrough
1Signing In & User Management
Access from Any Device — Secured by Google

The system is fully accessible from any device with a modern browser — a desktop computer, laptop, tablet, or smartphone. No software installation is required. Staff simply navigate to the site, tap "Continue with Google," and are signed in within seconds using their existing district account. Field staff on phones or tablets are automatically redirected to the optimized mobile dashboard.

Desktop — split-panel login with branding and Google Sign-In
Mobile login — district branding, one-tap sign in

Authentication is handled entirely by Google's OAuth 2.0 infrastructure — the system never receives, stores, or transmits district passwords. When a user signs in, Google verifies their identity and returns a secure token. The system then checks two things: (1) the account's email domain must end in @warrick.k12.in.us, and (2) the user must exist in the system with an active account. Any attempt to sign in with a personal Gmail, a different organization's account, or a deactivated district account is rejected before a session is ever created. If the district suspends a staff member's Google account, their work order system access is revoked automatically — no separate action needed from the administrator.

Managing Users — Administrator Access
Manage Users — user list with role filter tabs, live search, User Summary panel, and Add User modal showing building assignment checkboxes

Administrators access Manage Users from their profile avatar in the top-right corner. The page shows every account in the system, filterable by role tab and searchable by name or email. A User Summary panel on the right displays a live count of users in each role.

Creating a new account requires a first name, last name, district email address, and role assignment. For building-scoped roles (Building Principal, Building Technician, Building Custodian, Building Maintenance), a building picker appears — organized by High Schools, Middle Schools, and Elementary — allowing multi-building assignments in a single step.

Any existing user record can be edited inline — name, email, role, and buildings. Accounts can be deactivated (not deleted) at any time; deactivated users lose access immediately at their next login while all their historical order data remains intact.

2Roles & Permissions
Who Can See and Do What

Every user is assigned one of nine roles that controls exactly what they see and can act upon. Roles are set by an Administrator and take effect at the user's next login. Building-scoped roles are additionally filtered to one or more specific schools — those users never see orders from other buildings.

Role Title What They Can See & Do
A Administrator Full system access. Views, manages, approves, assigns, and closes all orders of both types across every building. Manages all user accounts, roles, and building assignments. Generates all report types. Accesses the Manage Users page to add, edit, or deactivate any account.
MM Maintenance Manager Sees all maintenance orders district-wide. Approves, rejects, assigns maintenance workers (MW, BC, BM), and marks orders complete. Generates maintenance-scoped PDF reports. Does not see technology orders.
MT Technology Manager Sees all technology orders district-wide. Approves, rejects, assigns building technicians (BT), and marks orders complete. Generates technology-scoped PDF reports. Does not see maintenance orders.
BP Building Principal Sees all orders (both types) submitted from their assigned building(s). Reviews escalated orders and can approve or reject them before they route to the department manager. Cannot assign workers or generate reports.
BT Building Technician Sees only technology orders assigned to them at their associated building(s). Adds notes and photos, marks assigned orders In Progress or Complete from the field. Has access to the mobile interface. Cannot approve, reject, or assign others.
BC Building Custodian Sees only maintenance orders assigned to them at their building. Adds notes and photos, updates status from the field via mobile. Scope is limited to their own assigned orders — cannot see other workers' orders or the full building queue.
BM Building Maintenance Identical capabilities to Building Custodian — sees only personally assigned maintenance orders at their building. Adds notes, attaches photos, and marks complete from the mobile interface. Building-scoped and assignment-scoped.
MW Maintenance Worker District-wide maintenance worker not tied to a specific building. Sees only orders assigned to them, regardless of which school the order originates from. Adds notes and photos from the field, marks work complete via the mobile interface.
U General User All teachers, office staff, and other district employees who are not in a specialized role. Submits maintenance and technology work orders. Sees only their own submitted orders and their current status. Receives email notifications as their orders progress. Cannot see other users' orders.
Building-Scoped Roles (BP, BT, BC, BM) When a building-scoped role is assigned, the administrator selects one or more school buildings during account creation. All queries for that user are automatically filtered server-side to those buildings — there is no way to accidentally view another school's orders.
Deactivating vs. Deleting Administrators deactivate accounts rather than delete them. This preserves the full history of orders that worker submitted or was assigned to. Deactivated accounts cannot log in but their names remain visible in historical activity logs and completed order records.

6Order Detail
Full Order Detail Page
Status & Priority Badges
Displayed in the top-right corner of every detail page, the status badge updates automatically as the order moves through the workflow (Pending → Approved → In Progress → Completed / Rejected). The priority badge reflects the current assigned level — authorized roles can change priority directly on this page, and the change is recorded in the Activity Log with a timestamp and the name of who made it.
Request Details Panel
The left-side panel displays all core information submitted with the order: building code, room or location, problem type, availability time window, the submitter's full name and email address, and the original submission date. This data is permanent and cannot be edited after submission — it forms the official record of what was requested and when.
Attached Photos & Description
The full description text appears in a dedicated block, followed by a thumbnail gallery of all attached photos. Clicking any thumbnail opens a full-screen lightbox viewer with left and right navigation arrows to step through multiple images. The lightbox uses browser history pushState so the Android back button and iOS swipe gesture close the viewer naturally without navigating away from the order.
Activity Log
A complete, timestamped audit trail of every action ever taken on the order: initial submission, approval or rejection decisions, priority changes and who made them, notes added by any staff member, worker assignments, and completion or rejection events. Each entry shows the date, time, actor name, role, and the specific action taken. The log is read-only and cannot be altered.
Assign Workers Panel
Displayed on the right side of the detail page, this panel shows a searchable list of eligible workers filtered by role and building. Managers check one or more names and click Assign — the system records the assignment, updates the status, and immediately sends an email notification to every assigned worker. The panel also reflects who is currently assigned and allows re-assignment if needed.
Action Buttons
The Actions section provides context-sensitive buttons based on the current status and the viewer's role. Approve → Principal escalates to the BP for review. Assign & Start assigns selected workers and moves the order to In Progress in one step. Mark Completed closes the order and notifies the submitter. Reject declines the order with a required written reason, also notifying the submitter by email.

7Taking Action
Assigning, Completing & Rejecting Orders
Order detail — In Progress, assigned workers John Knight, Chad McCombs, and Mike Kemp listed with full activity log below
Mark as Completed — confirmation dialog before closing the order
Reject Work Order — confirmation with order number; reason was provided earlier
Assign Workers Who can do it: Admin (A), Maintenance Manager (MM), Technology Manager (MT)
The Assign Workers panel on the detail page shows a filtered list of eligible staff — only workers in the correct role type for the order (MW/BC/BM for maintenance, BT for technology) are shown, and building-scoped workers are further filtered by building match. Managers check one or more workers and click Assign. Multiple workers can be assigned to a single order when a job requires a crew. Upon assignment, the order status advances to In Progress, the assigned workers immediately appear on the detail page under "Assigned Workers," and each worker receives an email notification directing them to the order. Workers can also be re-assigned or additional workers added at any time while the order remains open.
Mark as Completed Who can do it: Admin (A), Maintenance Manager (MM), Technology Manager (MT), assigned field workers
Clicking Mark Completed opens a confirmation dialog showing the order number and a plain-language summary of what will happen. Upon confirmation, the order status changes to Completed, the order is removed from active queues and moved to the Completed tab, and an email notification is automatically sent to the original submitter informing them their request has been resolved. The full history — all notes, photos, assigned workers, approval chain, and timestamps — remains permanently accessible in the order's activity log. Both field workers (from mobile) and managers (from desktop) can trigger completion.
Reject Order Who can do it: Admin (A), Building Principal (BP), Maintenance Manager (MM), Technology Manager (MT)
Rejecting an order requires a written reason before the action can be submitted — the system enforces this to ensure the submitter always understands why their request was declined. Upon confirmation, the order status changes to Rejected, the written reason is recorded in the Activity Log, and an email is sent to the original submitter that includes the rejection reason. Rejected orders remain visible under the Rejected filter tab for record-keeping and are never permanently deleted. If the underlying issue still needs to be addressed, the submitter can open a new work order — the previous rejected order serves as a reference.

8Print View & Reports
Branded Documentation & PDF Reporting Suite
Work order print view — WCSC-branded header, full request details, assigned workers, and complete activity log. Access via the Print button on any order detail page.
Reports panel — role-scoped drawer, six report types, Generate PDF button downloads immediately. Role determines which reports and which workers appear.
Active Maintenance Orders
A complete snapshot of every open maintenance work order in the system, sorted by priority (Urgent first) then by age. Each row includes building, room, problem type, submitter, currently assigned workers, current status, and the number of days the order has been open. Scoped to MM and Admin only — MM sees maintenance, Admin sees both types.
Active Technology Orders
The technology equivalent of the Active Maintenance report — all open technology service requests with building, room, problem category, submitter, assigned technician(s), and days open. Formatted identically for easy cross-department comparison. Accessible to Technology Manager and Admin. Neither manager sees the other's report type.
Aging Report
Surfaces orders that have been open beyond a configurable age threshold (default: 14 days). Designed to catch requests that are at risk of going unresolved. Each entry shows how many days the order has been open, color-coded by severity. Helps managers identify bottlenecks — whether in the approval chain, assignment, or active work. Available to Admin (both types), MM (maintenance only), MT (technology only).
By Building
Generates a full list of all work orders — both open and recently closed — for a selected school building. Useful for site visits, principal briefings, or end-of-year documentation for a specific facility. The administrator selects the building from a dropdown before generating. Provides a building-level view that no other report offers. Available to Admin only.
Staff Member Report
Focuses on a single selected employee and shows their current open work (assigned orders still in progress), their completion history over a chosen time period (30, 60, or 90 days or 1 year), and a count summary of their output. Designed for performance reviews, documentation conversations, and individual accountability tracking. Available to Admin (any staff member), MM (maintenance staff), MT (technology staff).
Workload Distribution
A multi-employee side-by-side snapshot comparing every worker's current open (assigned) orders against their completed orders over the selected period. Provides a bird's-eye view of how work is distributed across the team — useful for identifying overloaded workers, under-utilized staff, and overall department throughput. Supports data-driven decisions when re-assigning or hiring. Available to Admin (all staff), MM (maintenance staff), MT (technology staff).
Report Format — Branded, Instant, No Third-Party Software Every generated report is a formatted PDF document branded with the WCSC logo, organization name, and contact details in the header. Reports include a filter summary line, generation timestamp, the generating user's name, and a footer on every page. All documents download directly to the user's device — no external printing service or third-party software required.
